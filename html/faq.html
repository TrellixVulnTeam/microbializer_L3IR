Why my analysis results does not include a species tree?

The are several cases in which the analysis results will lack a phylogenetic tree. Generally, the data should contain at least 4 different genomic sequences and the inferred core proteome should not be empty.
  • Less than 4 genomes were provided (unrooted species tree for less than 4 genomes is trivial).
    Possible solution: provide more sequences.
  • Genomic sequences are too close (RAxML reduces the alignment to unique sequences, i.e., it removes duplicates).
    Possible solution: try to add more (evolutionary) distant species.
  • The species are too distant and the filtration parameters are too stringent (this might result in an empty core proteome).
    Possible solution: try to play with the running parameters values (under the "advanced options" yellow button).

Why does the homologs inference step take so long?

In the homologs inference step, each gene of each species is queried against all other genes in the data (except those who are related to the same species) making it the most computationally intensive step. Even though M1CR0B1AL1Z3R uses the fastest algorithms that are currently available, for a dataset with more ~250 genomes, this step might take even 10 hours. We are consistently working to make the pipeline faster (e.g., by parallelization).
Possible solution: be patient :)
